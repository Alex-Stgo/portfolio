A Markov decision process (MDP) is a discrete-time stochastic control process. It provides a mathematical framework for modeling decision making in situations where outcomes are partly random and partly under the control of a decision maker. MDPs are useful for studying optimization problems solved via dynamic programming. In this exercise, we will use the temporal difference with the Bellman equation to obtain the optimal route in a labyrinth to get from the starting to the ending point.
To maximize the Q-function we will use the argmax function.

Details

The main goal is to build an AI that will always take the shortest route to the top priority location, whatever the location it starts from.
